Future with will

Cómo formar, cuándo usarlo y en qué se diferencia de be going to.

1) Estructura básica

Afirmativa

Sujeto + will + verbo base

  • I will study tonight.
  • She will be a great engineer.
Contracción: I'll, you'll, he'll, she'll, it'll, we'll, they'll

Negativa

Sujeto + will not + verbo = won't + verbo

  • They won't arrive on time.
Nota: will not → won't (no *willn't*).

Interrogativa

Will + sujeto + verbo + ? — Will you help me?

2) Usos principales

  • Decisiones espontáneas (en el momento): I'm thirsty — I will get some water.
  • Ofertas y promesas: I will help you with your homework.
  • Predicciones basadas en opinión o creencias: I think it will rain later.
  • Avisos/amenazas: You will regret it.
  • Futuro inevitable o expectativas generales: People will live longer in the future.

3) Will vs. Be going to

SituaciónPreferibleEjemplo
Decisión tomada ahora (espontánea)willI'm cold — I will close the window.
Plan/intención previagoing toI am going to visit my grandma this weekend.
Predicción con evidencia visiblegoing toLook at those clouds! It is going to rain.
Promesa/ofertawillI will call you tonight.

4) Expresiones de tiempo frecuentes

tomorrow, next (week/month/year), in a minute/hour, soon, later, in the future

5) Respuestas cortas & etiquetas

  • Short answers: Yes, I will / No, I won't.
  • Question tags: She will pass, won't she? — They won't fail, will they?

6) Pronunciación & contracciones

  • will suele reducirse a sonido débil /wɪl/ o /l/ en habla rápida: I'll ≈ “ail”.
  • won't se pronuncia /woʊnt/.

7) Errores comunes

  • ❌ He will goes → ✅ He will go (el verbo va en base).
  • ❌ I won't to go → ✅ I won't go (sin “to”).
  • ❌ Will not = willn't → ✅ won't.

Ejemplos modelo

  • Predicción: I'm sure our team will win.
  • Promesa: I will always support you.
  • Decisión ahora: That’s heavy — I will carry it.
  • Oferta: Will you help me with this?
  • Plan previo (contraste): We are going to visit Bogotá next month.
TIP: con verbos de estado (believe, think, know) las predicciones suelen ir con will: I think it will be fine.

Mini‑quiz

1) Complete: “I think it ___ (rain) later.”

2) Decisión espontánea: “I'm hungry — I ___ a sandwich.”

3) Predicción con evidencia (nubes negras): “Look! It ___ soon.”

4) Short answer: “Will she pass the exam?” → Escribe Yes/No + sujeto + will/won't.

5) Corrige el error: “He will goes to school.”
